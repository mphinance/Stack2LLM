Back in the day, you could join a mining pool and mine Bitcoin on your laptop with your CPU. Every month or so you’d get your $1 or so payout - 0.003 - 0.005 BTC, or around $300-$400 today, even with $BTC TANKING.

Nowadays... not so much. Mining is much harder because the reward blocks are much smaller - cut in half less than every 2 years. So, costs go up, blah blah.

The numbers below are not going to be exact, but I’ve ran some things through AI, examined balance sheets and earnings reports, and these are the most accurate I can come up with. I’ll go Ticker, Direct Cost per BTC, and notes under.

$CIFR ~$35k per BTC (Direct Cost)

- Electricity cost only, but they are one of the most efficient being in Texas (hello Helio and $GLXY) and have some of the cheapest power contracts

$CLSK ~$45k

- Consistently one of the most efficient. They own their sites and upgrade machines aggressively to keep costs down

$WULF ~$45.5k

- Powerered by nuclear, larger R&D but lower per coin power long term

$RIOT ~$46.5k

- Also have massive scale in Texas. Also make revenue selling power back to the grid, subsidizing costs in lieu of efficient of others

$BITF ~$48k

- Direct cost is important here - corporate overhead & financials are still a mess after fiasco from firing CEO in 2024 (he turned around and sued). Either sleeper rocket if they can turn around, or will end up an acquisition

$HUT ~$58k

- Harder to get real numbers here, just a pure estimate. Note they are making more revenue converting those miners to HTPC, so wouldn’t call them a pure miner anymore

$CORZ ~$62k

- Again more difficult to actually calculate, but implied from Q3 revenue vs. cost. Also currently signing AI deals to convert miners into HTPC to hopefully save them from bankruptcy

$MARA is actually the most difficult, but also the largest. But it’d be like me telling you Amazon’s cost per item shipped.

MARA is on the same path as $MSTR where efficency doesn’t matter as much to them - only massive accumulation as fast as possible.

Gotten a helluva lot more expensive than my leftover laptops, that’s for sure.
