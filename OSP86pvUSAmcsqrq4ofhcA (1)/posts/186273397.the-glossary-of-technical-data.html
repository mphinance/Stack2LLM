Technical Analysis of Data Headers

• EMA8, EMA21, EMA34, EMA55, EMA89 This sequence represents the Exponential Moving Average Ribbon. Unlike Simple Moving Averages (SMAs), these apply a weighting factor to recent price data to reduce lag. The algo checks for Temporal Alignment or “Stacking.” A bullish stack requires the mathematical condition: EMA8 > EMA21 > EMA34 > EMA55 > EMA89. This confirms fractal trend alignment across short, medium, and long-term timeframes.

• ADX (Average Directional Index) This is a non-directional volatility derivative used to quantify the vector magnitude of a trend. It does not indicate direction (up/down) but rather velocity.

• Values < 20 indicate a mean-reverting or “choppy” regime (low velocity).

• Values > 20 confirm a structural trend is present (high velocity).

• The scanner filters for ADX > 20 to ensure trade entries align with significant momentum.

• dist_to_21 (Mean Reversion Delta) This metric calculates the percentage displacement between the current close price and the EMA21. In this model, the 21 EMA functions as the statistical mean or “fair value.”

• Low Delta: Price is near the mean (equilibrium). This is the “Action Zone” for risk-adjusted entries.

• High Delta: Price is extended (disequilibrium). Statistical probability favors a reversion to the mean rather than continuation.

• SqueezeRatio (Volatility Compression) This is the Bollinger-Keltner Coefficient. It measures the relationship between the width of the Bollinger Bands (2 standard deviations) and the Keltner Channels (Average True Range).

• Ratio < 1.0: The Bollinger Bands are inside the Keltner Channels. This indicates a period of extreme volatility compression (low variance), mathematically preceding a volatility expansion event (breakout).

• relative_volume_10d_calc (Flow Factor) A normalized scalar comparing the current session’s accumulated volume against a time-sliced 10-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) of volume.

• > 1.0: Above-average participation.

• > 2.0+: Statistical anomaly indicating institutional block activity or algorithmic execution. This validates price movement with liquidity conviction.

• ATR (Average True Range) A volatility metric that decomposes the asset’s trading range (High minus Low) over a specified period. It is used for normalization. It converts absolute dollar moves into “volatility units,” allowing for dynamic stop-loss placement and profit targets based on the asset’s specific noise floor rather than arbitrary percentages.

• Stoch_K / Stoch_D (Stochastic Oscillator) A momentum indicator that locates the current closing price relative to the high-low range over a set lookback period (usually 14).

• Stoch < 40: In a confirmed uptrend (Bullish EMA Stack), this signal indicates a “mature pullback.” It suggests the counter-trend selling pressure has exhausted, identifying a local inflection point,.

• TopWallStrike / TopWallOI (Gamma Exposure) This identifies the specific strike price with the highest aggregate Open Interest (OI) in the options chain.

• Gamma Magnet: Market makers (dealers) with short gamma exposure at these levels must hedge by trading the underlying asset. This creates a gravitational pull toward the strike (if price is approaching) or a pinning effect (if price is at the strike),.

• WallPosition A boolean or categorical logic gate determining the CurrentPrice vector relative to the TopWallStrike.

• BELOW: The Gamma Wall acts as overhead resistance (magnetic ceiling).

• ABOVE: The Gamma Wall acts as structural support (floor),.

• IV (Implied Volatility) Derived from the Black-Scholes pricing model, this reflects the market’s expected one-standard-deviation range for the asset’s price.

• High IV: Indicates high variance expectation. In the “Meme Screen,” stocks are sorted by this metric to identify assets with extreme speculative premium.

• Gross Margin / Rev Growth / FCF (Fundamental Filters) Used specifically in the “Small Cap Multibaggers” scan.

• Gross Margin: Revenue minus Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). High margins indicate pricing power.

• FCF (Free Cash Flow): Operating cash flow minus capital expenditures. Positive FCF validates the business model’s solvency independent of external financing.
