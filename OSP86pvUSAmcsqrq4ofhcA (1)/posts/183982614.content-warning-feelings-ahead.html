Additional Disclosure:

I use humor as a defense mechanism.

Much the same way a trader uses overly complicated option structures. Not because it prevents losses, but because taking reality fully unhedged can wipe you out.

This week has been… inefficient, to say the least.

When we lose one we love, it feels like a position you never wanted to exit getting closed without notice. The kind of holding whose value never shows up on a balance sheet, but somehow anchors the whole portfolio anyway.

When someone in our care is suddenly not okay, it’s like watching a trade you actually believed in get halted mid-session. You don’t panic. You can’t. You just sit there refreshing the feed, hoping the system comes back online and mercy finds some liquidity.

And sometimes it’s just paperwork. Long overdue. Filed more than once before. Not a surprise, not a crash, not even really sad. Just the formal close of a position that’s been flat, volatile, and emotionally expensive for a long time. Less heartbreak. More finality.

So if this reads like a wandering storyteller who learned finance from a questionable wizard, that’s intentional. This is hedging. This is me trying to scalp a little humor against a whole lot of reality.

Because if I don’t laugh, my brain starts issuing margin calls on things that were never meant to be collateral.

Here’s the quiet truth markets eventually teach, if you survive long enough.

You can manage risk and still take losses.
Losses are not indictments.
Some drawdowns are simply the cost of having been invested at all.

So I rebalance.
Trim exposure to regret.
Roll patience forward.
Hold the stuff that still pays dividends in meaning.

Still solvent.
Still sober.
Still here for the next candle.

Normal programming resumes shortly.
