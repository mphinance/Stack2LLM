Transparency is the only currency that matters here.

Most financial newsletters are opaque. You pay a fee, you get a PDF, and you have no idea if the author is actually trading their own ideas.

Momentum Phinance is different. I operate on a “Skin in the Game” model. When you subscribe, you aren’t just buying content; you are literally building the portfolio we track.

Here is exactly how the money flows and how the Phund grows based on how many of you decide to join me.

1. The Money Flow: From Stripe to Stocks

I charge $13/month.

Every dollar of Net Income (Revenue minus Substack/Stripe fees) is split down the middle.

  • 50% to Me/Business: Taxes, bills, life.

  • 50% to The Phund: Deposited directly into the public brokerage account.

The “Receipts” You Will See:

I’m not just going to tell you I invested; I’m going to show you the chain of custody.

  1. The Withdrawal: Screenshot of the payout from Stripe.

You will see the capital move from your subscription to our portfolio in real-time.

2. The Phund Architecture: The 50/50 Strategy

The capital inside the brokerage account is split evenly into two distinct strategies.

Side A: The Growth Engine (50% of Phund)

  • Goal: Long-term capital appreciation.

  • Holdings: A fixed basket of 15 stocks (5 Kings, 10 Knights).

  • Strategy: Value Averaging. We don’t just blindly buy. We use the monthly inflows to buy the assets that are underweight (i.e., buying the dip), constantly rebalancing towards our target allocation.

Side B: The Income Lab (50% of Phund)

  • Goal: Cash flow.

  • Strategy: The Wheel.

    • We hold cash to sell Cash Secured Puts (CSPs).

    • We collect premiums (income).

    • If assigned, we sell Covered Calls (CCs).

  • The Payout: I will withdraw these premiums to pay real-life bills. This proves the concept: Growth builds wealth; Income pays the rent.

3. The Scale: Unlocking the Playbook

Since I am committing a fixed percentage of revenue, the size of the fund depends entirely on the size of the community.

The strategy remains the same (High Volume, Weekly Options, Price Near Strike), but the targets get bigger as we grow.

Level 1: 100 Subscribers

  • Income Lab Budget: ~$275/month

  • The Target: Stocks trading under $3.00.

  • The Unlock: We can start wheeling the small stuff.

  • The Reality: It’s grinding, but it proves the concept. We sell the put, collect the premium, and if assigned, we wheel it.

Level 2: 250 Subscribers

  • Income Lab Budget: ~$700/month

  • The Target: Stocks trading in the $6 - $8 range.

  • The Unlock: Now we are moving into high-volume momentum favorites.

  • Why these? High liquidity and weekly options make these perfect for generating consistent premiums without locking up too much capital.

Level 3: 350 Subscribers

  • Income Lab Budget: ~$950/month

  • The Target: Stocks trading in the $8 - $10 range.

  • The Unlock: We step up to slightly more volatile, higher-premium plays.

  • The Strategy: At this level, the premiums start to look like real bill-paying money.

Level 4: 500 Subscribers

  • Income Lab Budget: ~$1,400/month

  • The Target: Stocks trading in the $11 - $15 range.

  • The Unlock: This is where we start trading household names.

  • The Shift: Selling puts here allows us to enter significant positions in companies we might actually want to hold long-term in Side A (Growth).

Level 5: 1,000 Subscribers

  • Income Lab Budget: ~$2,750/month

  • The Target: Stocks trading in the $20 - $30 range.

  • The Unlock: The mid-cap sweet spot.

  • Bonus: At this level, we also have the capital to start buying LEAPS (Long-Term Equity Anticipation Securities) on our highest conviction plays ($AVGO, $PLTR), adding leverage without the weekly stress.

4. The Selection Process

I don’t pick stocks by throwing darts. I use a system.

  1. The Screener: Every week, I run proprietary scanners looking for Momentum, Value, and Volatility anomalies.

  2. The Discussion: I bring the data to YOU. In the Paid Subscriber Chat, we discuss the candidates. You bring your bear cases; I bring my bull cases.

  3. The Decision: I make the final call. I execute the trade with our capital.

We are building a hedge fund in public, starting from zero.

Whether it’s $55 or $5,500, the discipline remains the same. We just get bigger toys.
