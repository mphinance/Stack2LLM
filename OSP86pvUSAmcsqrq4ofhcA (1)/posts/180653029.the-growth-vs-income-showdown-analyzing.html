Performance and Return Summary: Growth Still Wins the Race

Over the 15-month span from August 2024 to December 2025, QQQ delivered a commanding 27.23% Total Real Return. Let’s be clear: growth still wins, especially when the market is running. This number is the highest on the scoreboard and serves as the benchmark we must all strive to approach.

However, the income funds delivered a surprisingly strong challenge. The Nasdaq-focused enhanced-yield fund QQQI captured 25.08%, with JEPQ securing the third spot overall at 22.45%. This is significant because it confirms that you can generate substantial monthly cash flow without abandoning all upside potential, but the final, pure growth number from QQQ still stands apart.

The S&P 500 family followed a similar, albeit tighter, pattern. SPY logged 20.59%, while the two S&P income plays, TSPY and SPYI, trailed slightly at 17.95% and 17.89%, respectively. They provided the essential yield, but their total return profile was a couple of steps behind the QQQ group. The Year-to-Date (YTD) returns confirm this hierarchy, with QQQ leading at 18.06%, QQQI at 14.92%, and SPY at 13.40%.

Drawdown and Volatility Analysis: The Necessary Trade-off

Here is the inevitable, necessary trade-off for chasing that top-line growth number: risk. The data shows this explicitly in the drawdown metrics. The pure growth fund, QQQ, suffered the most brutal peak-to-trough drawdown of any fund in the study at -23.54%. You pay for the outperformance with serious volatility exposure.

Conversely, the funds that deliberately sacrifice some upside delivered stability. SPYI proved its shock absorption capabilities by limiting its worst drawdown to just -16.85%, with TSPY close behind at -18.43%. This confirms the option strategies applied to the S&P 500 base are highly effective for volatility containment.

The current market sentiment, as of December 2, 2025, further emphasizes this point: JEPQ was back at a 0% current drawdown, having fully recovered its recent turbulence. Meanwhile, QQQ was still carrying the largest current drawdown at -2.44%.

Income Winner: Why NEOS (SPYI, QQQI) Deserves the Crown

While JEPQ performed admirably, the real income winner here is the NEOS suite. SPYI achieved the lowest maximum drawdown of the entire enhanced-yield group (-16.85%) while still generating competitive returns. Crucially, NEOS’s use of index options offers superior tax treatment (Section 1256 contracts) on the options side of their income stream, a massive advantage that pure performance numbers don’t capture. Frankly, this level of tax-efficient income generation and drawdown control is why the older generation, like JEPI and its peers, no longer merit a detailed comparison in this category—the innovation is simply elsewhere.

The data is conclusive. While income strategies are essential for risk mitigation and providing dependable cash flow for those already married to a financial plan, the ultimate prize in total real return belongs to growth. The pursuit of pure growth, while volatile, simply prints a higher number over time.
