1. The Banking: Why Relay Beat Mercury for Compliance

The decision against Mercury was purely about automating compliance and implementing the Profit First accounting model without friction. This operational choice directly impacts the administrative efficiency and tax preparedness of your LLC.

The Profit First Mandate

For a trading LLC, separating funds is vital for clarity and tax compliance. The Profit First system requires immediately segregating incoming trading revenue into distinct, dedicated accounts, such as: Income, Owner’s Pay, Taxes, Operating Expenses, and Profit.

The Automation Difference

While both Relay and Mercury offer fee-free business checking and the ability to open multiple sub-accounts, only Relay provides the necessary automation to make the system function efficiently:

  • Relay’s Advantage: Relay is uniquely built for cash flow automation. It allows for up to 20 free checking accounts and, critically, supports percentage-based automatic transfers upon deposit. This means trading profits hitting the account are immediately and automatically split (e.g., 30% to Tax, 60% to Working Capital) in a single action.

  • Mercury’s Flaw: Mercury, while a great bank for tech startups, does not support this single-step, percentage-based allocation. It requires multiple, manual one-to-one transfers. For a busy LLC generating daily or weekly premiums, manual intervention introduces friction, potential errors, and unnecessary administrative work.

By choosing Relay, you automate the toughest part of running a profitable business: ensuring your Tax Reserve and Owner’s Pay are funded automatically.

2. The Brokerage: Why IBKR Pro Beat the Field

The decision against major brokers like Fidelity and Schwab wasn’t about the $0 commission; it was about the execution quality and platform power needed to efficiently execute the options-selling strategy.

The Capital Requirement Loophole

For an LLC starting with less than $1,000, IBKR Pro’s $0 account minimum is the only way to get institutional access.

  • You must be approved for Options Level 3 to execute the initial Short Put strategy, which is required for Cash-Secured Puts (CSPs). IBKR allows an Organizational Cash Account to apply for this level.

  • The capital constraint is not a broker-imposed fixed amount (like the $25,000 or $50,000 minimums required by competitors for advanced trading), but rather the cash required to secure the stock (Strike Price × 100). This allows the LLC to begin professional options selling immediately on low-priced stocks, a critical entry point the generalists often deny with their fixed, high equity floors.

Execution and Platform Power

Our discussions repeatedly favored IBKR’s dedication to professional traders over the retail-focused offerings, specifically in contrast to platforms like Fidelity’s Active Trader Pro (ATP):

  • PFOF vs. Smart Routing: Unlike brokers that rely on Payment for Order Flow (PFOF), IBKR uses SmartRouting to actively seek out the absolute best execution price across multiple exchanges, maximizing price improvement. When collecting premiums, this is a tangible difference that adds to the LLC’s profit.

  • Platform Speed and Power: IBKR’s Trader Workstation (TWS) is designed for professional volume. Platforms like Fidelity’s ATP are often reported by active traders to be clunky, laggy, or slow when compared to the raw processing power and advanced customization of TWS, where rapid order entry and reliable execution are paramount.

  • Advanced Tools and Data: TWS is built for scale, offering superior depth in tools for algorithmic trading, backtesting, and a much larger library of technical indicators. Even starting small, the LLC is securing a platform built for all future strategies.

The final combination ensures the LLC has the best tool for making money (IBKR) and the best tool for managing money automatically (Relay), all while starting with minimal seed capital.
