Cut the 6s, Double the As (my version of “Don’t Cut Your Flowers and Water Your Weeds)

This is the hardest part. You’re looking at a portfolio of red. It’s tempting to just hold everything and pray. Since I’m not giving financial advice I’m not gonna straight out say…Don’t, ;) so I’ll just say, instead, it’s definitely an option!

When I’m in an almost irrecoverable place, I look at my holdings and consolidate.

  • The “6s” (the mediocre stocks, the ones you bought on a whim): Cut them. Take the loss. Don’t cut the flowers.

  • The “As” (the high-conviction winners that got dragged down with the ship): Double down. Again, on the flowers. Not the weeds.
    Take the remaining capital from the losers and put it into the winners that actually have a shot at bringing you back to the surface.

Actionable Hedging: How to Stop the Bleeding

Time for tactical plans, even if you didn’t get it right before. Everyone tells you to “buy puts on SPY”! Great advice if you have unlimited capital and a time machine. Here are a few ways to hedge that aren’t just lighting money on fire.

Trading Against Your Book

This is psychological warfare against yourself, but it works.
If you are long Broadcom and it’s bleeding out, day trade puts on it.

“But I’m bullish long term!”
Who cares? You’re broke short-term.

Adopt the mindset: What’s the worst that can happen? (Do the next best thing)

Either your puts print and you make cash flow to survive today, or your long-term shares go up and your net worth heals. You have to balance the scales. Short-term losses for long-term gain. Survival. Going to rehab was the same yet opposite decision for me a couple years ago… short term capital loss so I could be alive to write this post today.
