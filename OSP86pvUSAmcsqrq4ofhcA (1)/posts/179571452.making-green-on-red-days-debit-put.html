The market is throwing a tantrum. My VIX calls are doing their job as the first aid kit, but I’m not one to just sit around and play defense while the house is on fire. I want to roast some marshmallows.

Buying straight puts right now? Expensive. When the market drops, Implied Volatility (IV) spikes. That means you are paying a premium for that fear. I prefer Debit Put Spreads (aka Bear Put Spreads) to get around this.

The Strategy:

  1. Buy a Put (usually ATM or slightly OTM) to catch the move down.

  2. Sell a Put at a lower strike (where you think the floor is or where you’d be happy taking profit).

Why I love this right now:

  • Cheaper Entry: The premium you collect from selling the lower put offsets the cost of the put you bought. You are financing your own downside bet.

  • Defined Risk: You can’t lose more than you paid for the spread. No naked risk here.

  • Theta Protection: Since you are selling an option too, you aren’t getting eaten alive by time decay as badly as a straight long put.

Example Math (Not financial advice, just numbers):

Stock is at $100. You think it’s going to $90.

  • Buy $100 Put for $5.00

  • Sell $95 Put for $3.00

  • Net Cost: $2.00 ($200)

If the stock tanks to $90? Your spread is worth $5.00. You paid $2.00. That is a 150% return on a $5 move.

Don’t try to catch a falling knife. Just bet on gravity.
