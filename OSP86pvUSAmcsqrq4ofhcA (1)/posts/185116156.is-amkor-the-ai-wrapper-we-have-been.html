Think of Amkor as the world’s most expensive gift wrapping service. Without their advanced packaging, an NVIDIA chip is just a very hot, very expensive paperweight.

The Financial Inflection Point

The third quarter of 2025 was a record breaker. Amkor delivered $1.99 billion in net sales, a 31% sequential increase that shattered expectations. More importantly, the revenue mix has shifted: Advanced Packaging now accounts for nearly 85% of total sales.

While legacy “Mainstream” products are dragging their feet due to a sluggish automotive sector, the Advanced segment grew 37% in a single quarter. This is the “AI premium” in the flesh.

The $7 Billion Bet on Arizona

Amkor is onshoring the AI technology stack, building a $7 billion campus in Peoria, Arizona. This is not just a factory: it is a landmark for U.S. supply chain security.

  • Timing: Construction ends in 2027; high volume production starts in early 2028.

  • The Anchor Customers: Apple and NVIDIA are officially on board.

  • The Ecosystem: By sitting right next to TSMC’s Phoenix fabrication site, Amkor creates a localized loop. No more sending chips on a round trip to Asia just to get them in a box.

New Leadership: The Engel Era

On January 1, 2026, Kevin Engel took over as CEO. A 20 year veteran and former COO, Engel is an operational specialist. His mission: balance the massive spending required for Arizona with the need to squeeze more profit out of underutilized facilities in Japan. Unlike most CEO transitions, this was a “warm handoff” during a period of strength, not a frantic mid-air engine repair.

The “Red Team” View: Every Party Has a Pooper

No investment is without friction, and Amkor has plenty of company at the top. The bear case rests on three pillars:

  1. The Heavyweight Competition: Amkor is the #2 player. They are constantly battling ASE Technology for market share, while foundry giants like TSMC are increasingly keeping the “packaging” profits for themselves.

  2. The Cash Flow Gap: Arizona is expensive. Amkor is spending roughly $950 million in CapEx, which means free cash flow is going to be tight through 2026.

  3. China Exposure: About 6% of 2026 sales involve shipping U.S. GPUs into China, a figure that is one bad headline away from being zero.
