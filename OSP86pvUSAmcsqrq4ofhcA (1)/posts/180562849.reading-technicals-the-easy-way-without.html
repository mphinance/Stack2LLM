I didn’t realize broke below $6 today allowing me to sell $5.50 puts, but I did grab 100 shares this evening to cover tomorrow instead. This post isn’t about the stock, but the technicals behind it.

Here’s the thing... the charts’ a storming mess. Just look at it and try and tell me which line is which.

The usual obvious answer to get technicals from is from Barchart, here:

https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/BBAI/technical-analysis

Thing is, it only lists the Simple Moving Averages. I do look at these on that page though:

  • Stoch %K: the 14 day is just above 40 at 43% so it didn’t hit my scanner, but I do look for pullbacks below 50 at minimum for entry

  • Average True Range, or ATR, (and related ADR) which is huge for this stock, for setting covered calls levels @MarketVictor has a great series on this somewhere. I use it for position sizing when trading futures as well.

  • ADX is below 20 so typically don’t want to be buying calls/LEAPS here - but wheeling on the other hand... that is perfectly acceptable. It’s also got more positive than negative direction, always a good thing.

Saving the real cheat sheet for last though... I didn’t realize how few use TradingView for anything besides charts, and you’re missing out.

https://www.tradingview.com/symbols/NYSE-BBAI/technicals/

3rd screenshot is from link above from Tradingview. Want to know what I see when I see a bunch of Sells when the price is above $6 and Buys when its below $6, regardless of the other details? It’s still the same mess of lines, just laid out differently.
