I’ve read a lot of books over the years, and wanted to summarize some of the lessons of money I’ve learned from them. Rich Dad, Poor Dad, 7 Habits of Highly Effective, Richest Man in Babylon, Intelligent Investor, little Buffett wisdom, and myself, follows.

Do yourself a favor and stop treating money like some holy grail goal. It is a tool, period. If you aren’t using it to build a wall around your life, you’re doing it wrong.

Money won’t build character - it amplifies the one you already are. While working on building wealth, work on becoming the person you want to be when you have it too - chasing it and hoping it’ll fix it leads to failure without luck. What it does is offer additional access for education, therapy, meditation - being saved from survival-mode stress.

  1. Money is a tool, not a goal. Use it to buy your freedom or it’ll just sit there doing nothing for you.

  2. Buy assets, not liabilities. If it doesn’t pay you to own it, don’t shove your cash into it. Stop buying junk that depreciates the second you drive it off the lot.

  3. Pay yourself first. I don’t care what your bills look like. You take your cut before the vultures get theirs.

  4. Diversify. Don’t put all your eggs in one basket unless you’re prepared to watch that basket 24/7.

  5. Control your spending. If you’re spending more than you make, you’re going to get burned.

  6. Invest in yourself. The best ROI isn’t in a ticker symbol. It’s in your own head.

  7. Time is your greatest asset. You can make more money, but you can’t buy more time. Stop wasting it on nonsense.

The 12 People You Need in Your Circle

Something something surrounding yourself with winners. If your circle is full of losers, you’re the next one in line.

Note: You don’t need 12 separate bodies taking up space. A lean circle is a strong circle. One person can (and should) wear multiple hats. Since I am married, I see this every day. Your spouse or your best partner might be your Listener, your Loyalist, and your Accountability Partner all in one. Don’t look for a crowd. Look for the right coverage.

  • The Mentor: Someone who has actually been there and done that. Not a paper trader. They give you the roadmap they already used.

  • The Visionary: The person who sees the cliff (or the massive breakout) before you drive over it. They keep you looking at the horizon.

  • The Challenger: You need someone to tell you when your trade idea is absolute garbage. They force you to defend your logic.

  • The Connector: The one who knows everyone. If you need an entry, they have the key.

  • The Accountability Partner: Someone to make sure you’re actually following your own damn rules and hitting your goals.

  • The Listener: Sometimes you just need to vent about a bad tick without getting “advice” from a novice.

  • The Motivator: When the market kicks your teeth in, they remind you why you’re in the game and get you back on your feet.

  • The Thinker: The analytical one who finds the signal in all the noise and helps you deconstruct the data.

  • The Realist: They keep your feet on the ground when you start thinking you’re a genius after one green day.

  • The Optimist: They see the opportunity and the silver lining when everyone else is panic selling.

  • The Protector: Someone who actually looks out for your interests and helps you stay safe from the vultures.

  • The Loyalist: The person who stays in the foxhole with you when the portfolio is bleeding red. They have your back, no matter what.

Habits of the Wealthy (No Fluff)

Wealthy people don’t gamble. They take calculated risks. It is about having an edge and keeping it.

  • Multiple Streams: Don’t rely on one paycheck. That is a single point of failure.

  • Live Below Your Means: If you’re “looking” rich, you’re probably actually broke. Real wealth is silent.

  • Hang with Winners: Success is contagious. So is failure. Choose who you eat with carefully.

  • Never Stop Learning: The second you think you know it all is the second the market takes your lunch money.

  • Take Calculated Risks: Shove in when you have the edge. Fold when you don’t.

  • Set Goals: If you don’t know where you’re going, you’ll end up exactly where the vultures want you.

  • Health is Wealth: You can’t spend your gains if you’re dead. Take care of the machine.
