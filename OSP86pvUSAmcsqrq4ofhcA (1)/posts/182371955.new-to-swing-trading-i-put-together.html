For those looking to practice without the heart-attack volatility of 2x/3x leverage, I am testing a strategy using Roundhill WeeklyPay ETFs ($NVDW, $PLTW, $AVGW).

The Strategy: Screener vs. Execution

One huge tip: Keep your screener focused on the underlying stocks (e.g., $NVDA, $PLTR).

  • The “Clean” Signal: Technical indicators like EMAs and RSIs are much “cleaner” on the actual stock charts than on synthetic ETFs.

  • The Execution: Once the underlying stock hits your setup, you buy the WeeklyPay ETF version to catch the move with 1.2x weekly leverage.

Why the Roundhill Weeklies?

  • Managed Heat: They target about 1.2x weekly performance. It gives you a little extra “juice” without the brutal daily-reset volatility of 2x/3x funds.

  • The “Wiggle Room” Dividend: These pay out cash every single week. It provides a mental safety net while you wait for the trade to play out.

The “Heads Up” for Newbies (Don’t Panic on the Drop)

Since these pay out every week, you need to know about the Ex-Dividend date (usually Monday).

  • The Drop: On the ex-dividend date, the ETF’s price will typically drop by the amount of the dividend paid.

  • Don’t Panic: If you see the price gap down on a Monday morning while the underlying stock is flat, check the dividend schedule first. That “loss” is just the cash being moved from the ETF price into your pocket.

Use the screener to find the trend on the big names, trade the Roundhill weeklies to manage the risk, and let the dividends handle the stress.
