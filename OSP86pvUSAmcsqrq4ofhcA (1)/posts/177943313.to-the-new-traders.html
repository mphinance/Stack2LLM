A friend of mine was talking about Thomas Jefferson earlier today, and it got me thinking about Jefferson’s letter to his nephew, Peter Carr. If you’re unfamiliar, Jefferson bestows a guide to life, education, and most famously - advice on questioning **everything**, including religion (very taboo at the time).

-------

You’re about to jump into the market. You’ll see my posts, you’ll see a thousand others. You’ll see screenshots of 500% gains, people calling the very top, and YOLOs that look like easy money. It all looks like a fast track to a new life.

It’s not.

So I’m writing you this one time with the only piece of advice that matters. It’s the same advice I give myself, and it’s the core of how I work: Do you own fucking research.

Question, with boldness and extreme bias, even my best ideas. If my trade is truly good, it will stand up to your own analysis. If it doesn’t, it was never a good trade for you.

Don’t be a sheep. I post my ideas based on my own analysis, my own indicators, and my own experience. I’m just a guy on an app. I’m early, I’m late, I’m wrong. Following me or anyone else *blindly* is the fastest way to get your account blown to hell.

So how do you build that conviction?

Don’t just read the “news” or the “whale alerts.” That’s dogma. That’s just what someone else wants you to think. There is only one source that matters here, the primary one - the chart. The candles. The mother fucking MONEY, people, it’s the only language that matters.

Treat the chart as your scripture. Learn to read it like a book. Is it respecting the 8 EMA? Is it bouncing off the 50 SMA? Where is the volume? Is it real, or is it just algos playing games in premarket?

Look at the option chain for a good source of truth. Where is the open interest stacking up? Is the IV juiced to 190% before earnings? If it is, buying that call is a sucker’s bet. The “casino” (ME!) is selling that premium, and you should be, too. Learn what “theta” is and why it’s the most powerful force in the market.

The “wheel” that I post about a lot is not a lotto ticket - it’s a *process*; a strategy build on margin of safety and income. If you see me post a YOLO, you better believe that’s <1% port allocation, not the whole damn thing. Most people just post the win (loss), not the position size. Yeah I lost money on $AIIO but has it made it so I can’t feed my kid?

Virtue in this game isn’t about being right 100% of the time. It’s about *surviving*. A good trader isn’t the one who never loses; it’s the one who knows exactly how much they *can* lose before they even enter the trade.

So, do your own DD. Trust the charts, not the hype. Build a process. Control your risk.

If you do that, you’ll be secure. Whether any single trade works out or not, you’ll still be in the game.

-MPH
