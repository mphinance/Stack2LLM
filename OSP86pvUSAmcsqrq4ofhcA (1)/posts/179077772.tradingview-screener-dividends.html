Alright, I’ve spent some time refining my dividend screener and got it to a point where it’s filtering out most of the high-yield junk and finding quality names.

Here are the key filters I’m running and why:

  • Div yield, TTM: 2.7% to 9% This is my main “no-crap” filter. The 9% cap is key to avoiding those 15%+ yield traps that are likely to cut. The 2.7% floor just cleans out the noise.

  • Debt/Equity, FY: < 2 Simple balance sheet check. I don’t want to own any company that’s drowning in debt.

  • Cont. div growth: > 5 I want companies that are growing their dividend, not just paying a stagnant one. This shows a healthy, growing business.

  • Analyst Rating: Buy & Strong Buy A basic quality check. I can always loosen this to Neutral if the list is too small.

  • P/FCF: < 20 This is the real safety filter. “Price to Free Cash Flow” is my proxy for a cash flow payout ratio. Setting it < 20 is a backdoor way of ensuring the FCF Yield is at least 5% (1 / 20 = 0.05), which helps confirm the dividend is actually covered by cash.

Columns for Manual Review

I’m also showing a few key columns that I review manually, most importantly Div payout ratio % TTM. I don’t filter on this because it’s broken. It’s useless for $REITs (like $ADC, $LXP) where it’ll show 150%+, and it makes healthy cyclicals ($CVX, $PSX) look terrible if they had one bad earnings quarter. I leave it on so I can see a flag (like $UPS at 101%) and know to investigate it myself.

I also added ROCE as a column. My buddy @TheAnalyst was telling me about this one. It’s a great “quality” metric to see how good a company is at turning its capital (debt + equity) into profit. A high, stable ROCE usually signals a strong business.

The list it’s kicking out now looks solid. Seems to be finding a good mix of quality and yield.
