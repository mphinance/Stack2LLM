The first type is the one we’re all familiar with - these institutions are the biggest, the most popular, provide the market with liquidity, and are the ones many traders blame for “manipulation”.

The Market Makers

  • Used by: Citadel, Susquehanna

  • Goal: Profit from volatility and arbitrage - not direction

  • The Concept: When you buy yolo options, I sell them to you. Now I must buy shares to get back to delta neutral

The Mechanics (How it burns retail)

  • Stock rises, so the calls I sold you are losing money. Now I’ve gotta buy more stock to hedge, adding fuel to the fire (gamma squeeze).

  • Retail started taking some profits after it blew my strike, but now the stock is stalling and I’ve got a lot of shares. Time to start selling to get back to neutral

  • Retail is cursing me for manipulation because they kept buying the top and buying my premiums and now I have to take profit. Boo hoo

  • We can start buying contracts for $5 and selling for $7 on the spread - retail is buying thousands at a time.

The second type sell the “event fade”, and use retail enthusiasm as exit liquidity. These are the ones we pretend to hate but need..

The Short Sellers

  • Used by: Event-Driven Pods, Prop Desks

  • Goal: Profit from retail

  • The Concept: Retail reacts to news 3-5 days late. We buy before the news, and sell into it

The Mechanics (How it burns retail)

  • My junior analyst told me the CEO of $ONDS is interviewing with retail Friday night. Let’s start accumulating along the TWAP (Time Weighted Average Price)

  • ONDS just released the interview and it was a hit - retails’ buying like crazy!

  • Let’s not dump all at once and crash the price. We need Iceberg Orders (hidden sell walls)

  • We can start buying contracts for $5 and selling for $7 on the spread - retail is buying thousands at a time (Visual: You see a “Ask” size of 100 shares at $12.50. You buy it. Immediately, another 100 appears. And another. They are feeding you shares without moving the price up)

The third type doesn’t care if the S&P 500 goes to the moon or crashes to zero. While you are betting on a “moon mission,” these guys are playing a math game where they strip away the market’s noise to find the signal. I like to call this The Pairs Trade.

This particular type of trading is also closest to how I try and hedge my own portfolios. is one of my favorite stocks, but if crypto or small caps go through winter, I don’t want to be left out freezing the whole time.

This is removing Beta (market dynamics) to leave only Alpha.

The Quants (Market Neutral / Multi-Strategy Pods)

  • Used by: Millennium, Point72, Balyasny

  • Goal: Pure Alpha (Isolating the specific move, removing the market’s influence)

  • The Concept: We don’t want “Beta” (market direction). We use “Pair Trades” to cancel out the sector. If we think one company is better than its peers, we buy the winner and short the loser (or a sector ETF) in equal amounts. Now, we only profit if our stock outperforms the other. The “market” is removed from the equation.

The Mechanics (How it burns retail)

  • We like but we’re scared of a Bitcoin crash. We buy GLXY and (short Bitcoin ETF). If the whole crypto market tanks 20% but GLXY only 15%, we actually made a profit on the spread.

  • We saw a catalyst for but think is overextended. We buy $KTOS and short $IWM (Russell 2000). If $IWM stays flat and $KTOS moves up 2%, we win. If $IWM drops 5% and $KTOS drops 3%, we still win. Retail is confused why $KTOS is “holding up” while the market dies. It’s because we are the ones buying the $KTOS leg of our hedge.
