I was recently introduced to Best Loser Wins by Tom Hougaard (thank you @Locker) It claims to be a book on trading, disguised as psychology. As I was reading I found myself nodding - not because I have mastered anything in the market, but because I’d already survived something far more cunning: myself. This post is selfishly mostly for my own self reflection, and has zero financial advice, so I’ll just say this was a great, rather, a phenomenal book.

Every line about discipline, fear, and sitting while the world tilts? I’ve heard that sermon before. Not from a broker in a suit, but from the ragged edge of addiction, and the slow, stubborn climb of recovery. Jelly Roll’s music can make you feel this far better than I can ever explain.

Anyway, turns out, when you’ve learned how to lose everything and still show up the next day - you already know the secret. You’ve just been calling survival, rather than strategy, up until now.

ADDICTION AND RECOVERY TAUGHT ME TO BE THE BEST LOSER

1. Emotional Regulation
In recovery, I learned to sit with some deeply uncomfortable feelings—cravings, guilt, boredom, raw fear—and not immediately self-destruct in response.
Now, when I’m trading and the chart turns red, I use the same skill. I stay calm. I don’t flinch or chase. I just breathe and follow my plan.

2. Discipline & Routine
Recovery wasn’t about big breakthroughs—it was about showing up every day, especially when I didn’t want to.
Trading works the same way. I don’t chase highs. I follow structure. I trust the process, even when it’s boring.

3. Self-Awareness
Addiction forced me to get brutally honest. I started spotting the lies I told myself—“just one more,” “I’m fine.”
Turns out, traders lie to themselves too. “This trade will turn around.” “I’ll just double down.”
Recovery gave me the superpower to call out my own BS.

4. Tolerance for Discomfort
Most people chase comfort like it’s a birthright. I’ve learned that comfort can be a trap.
Recovery taught me how to stay put in discomfort—how to hold its hand and keep walking anyway.

5. Letting Go of Control (perhaps should be #1)
Recovery taught you that control is a myth. You can’t control the world—only your response to it.
Same in trading. I can’t control the market, but I can manage my risk. That’s not weakness. That’s wisdom hard-earned.

I am the biggest loser I know, and today I can be grateful for it. My greatest enemy is cunning, baffling, & powerful, and has beaten me more times than I can count. But every time I’ve lose, I’ve learned. Every time I’ve hit the floor... it was usually slower, but I did stand up stronger.

Fall. Lose. Adapt. Then WIN.
